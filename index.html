Section 1 of 3

Revenue Organization KPIs

"Is the revenue engine healthy?"

Revenue Org Deal Size (ACV / ASP)

What It Measures

Average revenue per closed deal

How to Calculate

Total closed revenue ÷ number of closed deals

Benefit

Shows whether reps are moving upmarket and selling value

Downfall

Can be inflated by a few outlier deals; doesn't reflect volume

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Larger deals often increase deal cycle length and decrease win rate. Must be read alongside both.

Revenue Org Win Rate

What It Measures

Percentage of opportunities that close

How to Calculate

Closed-won deals ÷ total deals worked (won + lost)

Benefit

Clearest signal of sales effectiveness and deal qualification

Downfall

Easily gamed by sandbagging pipeline or only logging sure-things

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Low win rate + high pipeline may signal a qualification problem. High win rate + low pipeline means reps aren't prospecting enough.

Revenue Org Deal Cycle Length

What It Measures

Average days from opportunity creation to close

How to Calculate

Sum of days to close all won deals ÷ number of won deals

Benefit

Reveals friction in the sales process; shorter cycles = faster revenue

Downfall

Varies heavily by segment (SMB vs. enterprise); averages can mislead

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Longer cycles often correlate with larger deal sizes. Shortening cycle without watching win rate can mean rushing deals.

Revenue Org Retention / Churn Rate

What It Measures

Percentage of customers retained (or lost) over a period

How to Calculate

Retention: (customers at end − new customers) ÷ customers at start. Churn: customers lost ÷ customers at start.

Benefit

Measures whether what's being sold actually sticks post-sale

Downfall

Lagging indicator — by the time it moves, damage is done

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

High churn + high win rate can mean reps are overselling or misqualifying. Connects directly to deal size if churned customers are concentrated in a segment.

Revenue Org Number of Opportunities

What It Measures

Total open pipeline count

How to Calculate

Count of all active opportunities in CRM

Benefit

Top-of-funnel health check; early warning system for future quarters

Downfall

Quantity without quality is meaningless — junk pipe inflates this number

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Must be paired with pipeline coverage ratio and win rate. High opportunity count with low win rate = qualification problem.

Revenue Org Pipeline Coverage Ratio

What It Measures

How much pipeline exists relative to quota

How to Calculate

Total pipeline value ÷ quota target (target: 3x+)

Benefit

Tells you if there's enough pipeline to absorb losses and still hit the number

Downfall

Misleading if pipeline is stale or full of stuck deals

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Coverage ratio only matters if win rate and deal cycle are healthy. 5x coverage with 10% win rate is worse than 3x with 35%.

Revenue Org Revenue Per Rep

What It Measures

Average revenue generated per quota-carrying rep

How to Calculate

Total revenue ÷ number of quota-carrying reps

Benefit

Best single measure of sales productivity; useful for headcount planning

Downfall

Doesn't distinguish between ramping reps and tenured reps

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Pairs with quota attainment distribution — revenue per rep can look healthy while being carried by a few top performers.

Revenue Org Quota Attainment Distribution

What It Measures

How quota performance is spread across the team

How to Calculate

Bucket reps by % of quota hit (e.g., 0-50%, 50-100%, 100%+) and calculate % of team in each bucket

Benefit

Reveals whether success is broadly distributed or concentrated in a few stars

Downfall

Doesn't explain why reps are underperforming

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

If revenue per rep is high but distribution is skewed, enablement impact is narrow. Broad attainment = scalable sales machine.

Revenue Org Ramp Time to Full Productivity

What It Measures

How long it takes a new hire to hit full quota

How to Calculate

Average number of months from start date until a rep hits 100% of prorated quota

Benefit

Directly measures onboarding and enablement effectiveness

Downfall

Definition of "ramped" varies — must be standardized across the org

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Faster ramp feeds revenue per rep and quota distribution. Long ramp + high attrition = massive cost problem.

Revenue Org Forecast Accuracy

What It Measures

How close the forecast is to actual results

How to Calculate

(Forecasted revenue − actual revenue) ÷ actual revenue, expressed as % variance

Benefit

Builds leadership trust; signals pipeline discipline and deal inspection rigor

Downfall

Can incentivize sandbagging (under-forecasting to always "beat")

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Poor forecast accuracy often traces back to weak stage conversion data and inconsistent deal qualification.

Revenue Org Stage Conversion Rates

What It Measures

Percentage of deals that advance from one pipeline stage to the next

How to Calculate

Deals that moved to stage N+1 ÷ total deals that entered stage N

Benefit

Pinpoints exactly where deals fall out of the funnel

Downfall

Only useful if pipeline stages are well-defined and consistently applied

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Feeds win rate (the final conversion), deal cycle, and forecast accuracy. The most diagnostic metric on this list.

Section 2 of 3

Team KPIs

"Which teams need the most support and where?"

Team Average Deal Size by Team

What It Measures

Whether certain teams are consistently landing bigger or smaller deals

How to Calculate

Total closed revenue for team ÷ number of closed deals for team

Benefit

Identifies which teams are selling value vs. discounting to close

Downfall

Segment differences (SMB vs. mid-market) make direct comparison unfair without normalization

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Teams with higher deal size often have longer cycles and need different enablement support than high-volume teams.

Team Win Rate by Team / Segment

What It Measures

Close rate broken down by team or market segment

How to Calculate

Team's closed-won deals ÷ team's total deals worked

Benefit

Reveals whether certain teams have a skill gap, process gap, or market fit gap

Downfall

Small sample sizes on newer or smaller teams make this noisy

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Compare against deal size and discount rate — a team with high win rate but high discounting is buying deals, not winning them.

Team Pipeline Generated Per Rep

What It Measures

How much new pipeline each team member is creating

How to Calculate

Total new pipeline created ÷ number of reps on team

Benefit

Measures prospecting and sourcing activity at the team level

Downfall

Doesn't distinguish between self-sourced and marketing/SDR-sourced pipeline

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Low pipeline per rep + high win rate = efficient but at-risk team. High pipeline per rep + low win rate = activity without quality.

Team Stage Conversion Rates by Team

What It Measures

Where in the funnel each team is losing deals

How to Calculate

Team's deals advancing to stage N+1 ÷ team's deals that entered stage N

Benefit

Pinpoints exactly which sales motion step is breaking for a specific team

Downfall

Requires clean CRM data and consistent stage definitions across teams

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

The most actionable team metric — drives targeted enablement (discovery training, demo coaching, negotiation skills) based on where the leak is.

Team Deal Velocity

What It Measures

Speed at which pipeline converts to revenue

How to Calculate

(Number of opportunities × win rate × average deal size) ÷ deal cycle length

Benefit

Single metric that captures pipeline health, efficiency, and speed in one number

Downfall

Composite metric — hard to know which lever to pull when it moves

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Combines four other metrics. Use it as a summary scorecard, then drill into the components to diagnose.

Team Ramp Time by Cohort

What It Measures

Whether onboarding improvements are actually working

How to Calculate

Average months to full productivity for each onboarding class/cohort

Benefit

Directly measures enablement program improvement over time

Downfall

Confounded by market conditions, territory quality, and hiring bar changes

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Improving ramp time should flow into better revenue per rep and quota attainment distribution over time.

Team Revenue Per Rep by Tenure

What It Measures

How experience level correlates with output on each team

How to Calculate

Segment reps by tenure (0-6 mo, 6-12 mo, 12+ mo) and calculate average revenue per bucket

Benefit

Reveals whether the team develops reps over time or plateaus

Downfall

Survivorship bias — underperformers leave, inflating tenured rep numbers

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Flat revenue growth by tenure suggests a coaching or enablement gap. Pairs with ramp time to show the full rep lifecycle.

Team New Hire Attrition Rate

What It Measures

Percentage of new hires that leave within 12 months

How to Calculate

New hires who left within 12 months ÷ total new hires in the cohort

Benefit

Early attrition is one of the most expensive problems — signals hiring, onboarding, or culture issues

Downfall

Doesn't distinguish between voluntary and involuntary attrition

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

High attrition + long ramp time = compounding cost. Every rep who leaves before ramping is a pure loss.

Team Competitive Win Rate

What It Measures

How the team performs against specific competitors

How to Calculate

Deals won against competitor X ÷ total deals where competitor X was present

Benefit

Identifies whether teams need competitive battlecard training or positioning help

Downfall

Requires reps to consistently log competitors in CRM, which they often don't

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Low competitive win rate + healthy overall win rate means the team avoids competitive deals. That may be smart — or it may be a blind spot.

Team Discount Rate / Margin Erosion

What It Measures

How much the team discounts off list price to close

How to Calculate

Average: (list price − sold price) ÷ list price across team deals

Benefit

Shows whether the team is selling on value or competing on price

Downfall

Some discounting is strategic (land-and-expand); blanket "reduce discounts" can hurt

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

High discount rate + high win rate = buying deals. High discount + short cycle = reps skipping negotiation. Pair with deal size to see full impact.

Section 3 of 3

Individual KPIs

"Where is this rep leaking deals?"

Individual Pipeline Generated

What It Measures

Total new pipeline a rep creates in a period

How to Calculate

Sum of opportunity values created by the rep in a given period

Benefit

Shows whether a rep is self-sourcing or relying entirely on inbound/SDRs

Downfall

Reps can inflate by creating low-quality or aspirational opportunities

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Pipeline generated only matters if activity-to-opportunity conversion is also healthy. High pipe + low conversion = junk.

Individual Stage-to-Stage Conversion Rates

What It Measures

Where in the funnel this specific rep is losing deals

How to Calculate

Rep's deals moving to stage N+1 ÷ rep's deals entering stage N

Benefit

The most precise diagnostic for individual coaching — pinpoints the exact skill gap

Downfall

Small deal counts per rep can make percentages unreliable in short timeframes

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

A rep with low discovery-to-demo conversion needs different coaching than one with low proposal-to-close conversion. Drives targeted enablement.

Individual Average Deal Size

What It Measures

The average value of deals a rep closes

How to Calculate

Rep's total closed revenue ÷ rep's number of closed deals

Benefit

Reveals whether the rep is selling the full solution or landing small and never expanding

Downfall

Segment and territory assignment heavily influence this — not always a skill issue

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Low deal size + high win rate can be fine (volume play) or a problem (not selling value). Pair with discount rate to differentiate.

Individual Win Rate

What It Measures

Percentage of the rep's opportunities that close

How to Calculate

Rep's closed-won deals ÷ rep's total deals worked

Benefit

Direct measure of closing ability and deal qualification skills

Downfall

Cherry-picking easy deals or sandbagging pipeline inflates this

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Must be read with pipeline generated — a 50% win rate on 4 deals is very different from 30% on 40 deals.

Individual Deal Cycle Length

What It Measures

How long the rep takes to close deals

How to Calculate

Average days from opportunity creation to close for the rep's won deals

Benefit

Identifies reps who move deals efficiently vs. those with stalled pipelines

Downfall

Reps with larger deal sizes will naturally have longer cycles — compare within segment

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Compare against time in stage to find where deals are getting stuck. Long cycle + healthy stage conversion = complex deals, not a problem.

Individual Activity-to-Opportunity Conversion

What It Measures

What percentage of meetings/calls become real pipeline

How to Calculate

Opportunities created ÷ total meetings/discovery calls held

Benefit

Measures whether the rep's prospecting and qualification is effective

Downfall

Doesn't capture the quality of the opportunities created

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Low conversion here wastes the top of funnel. If pipeline is high but conversion is low, it's a qualification problem.

Individual Multi-Threading Depth

What It Measures

How many contacts a rep engages per deal

How to Calculate

Average number of contacts with activity on closed-won deals vs. closed-lost deals

Benefit

Single-threaded deals are fragile — multi-threading is the strongest predictor of deal health

Downfall

Requires reps to log contacts accurately; not always reliable in CRM

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Low multi-threading + long deal cycle = deals stalling because the champion can't sell internally. Directly affects win rate on enterprise deals.

Individual Discount Rate

What It Measures

How much the rep discounts to close deals

How to Calculate

(List price − sold price) ÷ list price per deal, averaged

Benefit

Shows whether the rep can hold pricing and sell on value

Downfall

Not always the rep's fault — procurement pressure, competitive pressure, and approval processes matter

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

High discounting + fast cycle = skipping negotiation. High discounting + high win rate = buying deals. Pair with deal size to see total revenue impact.

Individual Time in Stage

What It Measures

How long deals sit at each pipeline stage

How to Calculate

Average number of days deals spend in each stage before advancing or dying

Benefit

Identifies exactly where deals are stalling — more precise than overall cycle length

Downfall

Meaningful thresholds differ by stage (discovery takes less time than negotiation)

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

The diagnostic layer underneath deal cycle length. High time at discovery = not qualifying fast enough. High time at negotiation = needs pricing/objection handling help.

Individual Talk-to-Listen Ratio

What It Measures

How much time the rep talks vs. listens on calls

How to Calculate

Pulled from conversation intelligence tools (Gong, Chorus) — rep talk time ÷ total call time

Benefit

Proxy for whether the rep is running real discovery or doing a feature dump

Downfall

Ideal ratio varies by call type (discovery vs. demo vs. negotiation)

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

High talk ratio + low discovery-to-demo conversion = the rep isn't asking enough questions. Low talk ratio doesn't automatically mean quality.

Individual Demo-to-Proposal Conversion

What It Measures

Whether demos lead to next steps

How to Calculate

Proposals sent ÷ demos delivered

Benefit

Directly measures demo effectiveness — are demos creating momentum?

Downfall

Can be inflated by sending proposals to unqualified prospects

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Low demo-to-proposal + good discovery conversion = the demo itself is the problem. If proposals go out but don't close, it's a pricing or negotiation issue.

Individual Expansion / Cross-Sell Revenue

What It Measures

Revenue generated from existing customers (for AMs)

How to Calculate

Total expansion revenue ÷ total book of business (net revenue retention from rep's accounts)

Benefit

Measures whether AMs are growing accounts or just maintaining them

Downfall

Expansion potential varies wildly by account — some accounts simply have no room to grow

How It Interacts With Other Metrics

Low expansion + high retention = stable but stagnant. High expansion + high churn on other accounts = focused on easy upsells, ignoring at-risk accounts.
